# Launch pledge

**Status:** approved draft — the owner posts this personally; it is not site copy.
**Proposed post date:** Tuesday, September 22, 2026 (inside the government-funding-fight
news window, ahead of the Sept 30 press moment — see
`docs/ideation/2026-07-05-build-gtm-strategy.md`, Phase 3).
**Where:** somewhere costly to walk back — LinkedIn, X, or the founder's personal site.

## The pledge

> On September 22, I'm publicly launching Oravan (oravan.org) — a free, nonpartisan
> tool that helps anyone in the US understand what Congress is actually voting on,
> in plain English or Spanish, and call their representatives about it. No accounts,
> no tracking, no ads. It's live now; on the 22nd I start telling everyone.
> My target: **1,000 constituent calls logged by November 3.** Hold me to it.

The two load-bearing parts are the **date** and the **number** — tweak everything
else freely.

## Why a dated public pledge

- It rides the funding-fight earned-media window instead of launching into silence.
- "1,000 logged calls by Nov 3" is the usage metric grant applications ask for
  (counted client-side, privacy-clean — no server-side user data).
- Posting it publicly with a date makes the launch a commitment, not an intention.

## Related work (not part of the pledge itself)

- A public calls tracker on the site, updated toward the Nov 3 goal, is called for
  by the GTM strategy (Phase 3 checklist) and is not yet built.
- A Spanish version of the post can be drafted if the owner wants to post bilingually.
