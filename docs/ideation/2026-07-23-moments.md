# Oravan Moments — topical entry pages (design document, no code)

**Status:** exploration → proposed shape, awaiting owner review. **Owner:** solo builder; every moment is owner-approved before it ships. **Constraint stack:** zero server-side user data, bilingual parity, nonpartisan by construction, AI labeled + human-reviewed before it drives a call, static-first, accessibility, ~zero marginal spend.

---

## 0. One-paragraph shape

A **Moment** is a small, curated, statically generated page that meets a visitor where the news left them — "the Iran war-powers question," "the shutdown fight" — and routes them in one hop to the objective legislative vehicles that fight actually runs through: the H.Con.Res. war-powers resolution, the CR, the rescission package, the appropriations bill. The founding insight: **most "non-legislation" call topics ARE legislation — the gap is topical discovery, not data.** The corpus already tracks hr/s/hjres/sjres/hconres/sconres and the pipeline already ingests tier-0 government signals (House/Senate floor-today feeds, the House Clerk's bills-this-week schedule, Congress.gov weekly most-viewed — `scripts/newsdesk.mjs`, 2026-07-23). A moment adds no new data and no new AI surface: it is a hand-curated, criteria-gated index entry over bills that exist, decoded, with working call scripts. At most a handful exist at once; each dies honestly when its fight settles.

---

## 1. Problem statement

- **Visitors arrive with a story name, not a bill number.** Nobody types "hconres-45-119"; they type "Iran," "the shutdown," "war powers." Site search today is a client-side substring filter over titles, decoded headlines, and tag names (`components/BillsBrowser.tsx`) — it hits only when the story's vocabulary happens to appear in a decoded headline.
- **The press-nickname gap is proven, not hypothesized.** The 2026-07-23 newsdesk incident recorded in `scripts/newsdesk.mjs`'s own header: the week's biggest bills (NDAA H.R.8800, the CR, the SAVE America Act) never triggered from headlines because mainstream coverage uses nicknames, not citations. Tier-0 government feeds closed that gap for the *pipeline*; nothing yet closes it for the *visitor*.
- **The bills feed answers "what's moving," not "where is the thing I heard about."** Urgency bands are the right default view, but a visitor activated by one specific story has to recognize their story inside a ranked list of decoded headlines. Many bounce at exactly the moment they were readiest to call.
- **The cost of the gap is the product's whole promise:** a nervous first-timer with five minutes either finds the vehicle in one hop or concludes "there's no bill about this" — which is usually false.

## 2. The concept — and what it is not

A moment is **a routing page, not a content product**: a neutral name for a live legislative question, a short plain-language "what Congress is actually deciding," and cards for each vehicle linking to its existing bill page and script flow. Everything below the moment's own summary is the existing product.

**Non-goals (each one structural, not aspirational):**

| Never | Because |
|---|---|
| **An issue-advocacy page** | A moment names a *question Congress is deciding*, never a cause. "The Iran war-powers question," not "Stop the war" / "Support our allies." Enforced by the naming grammar and vocabulary rules in §3.3, in both languages. |
| **A stance** | The page never recommends, pre-selects, or weights a position. Every vehicle links to the existing action panel where support and oppose scripts are equally one tap away. Act links never carry a stance parameter. |
| **A moment without a real vehicle** | No corpus bill in a callable state → no moment, no matter how loud the news cycle. "Congress isn't voting on this" is a finding the site expresses by *absence*, never by an empty topical page that implies otherwise. |
| **A news product** | No dated update stream, no "developing" ticker, no editorializing about momentum. Freshness on a moment page derives entirely from the underlying bills' statuses and the existing sync freshness stamp — the same read-time recomputation discipline as urgency itself. Third-party coverage stays on bill pages (existing Read section); moments don't re-render it. |
| **A permanent taxonomy** | Moments are not categories (that's `lib/taxonomy.ts`'s 12). They are born from a specific live fight and retire when it settles. A cap keeps them scarce (§4.3). |

## 3. Curation rules — the heart

### 3.1 Objective qualification: ALL of the following, checkable, before a moment may exist

1. **Vehicle rule.** ≥1 bill in `data/bills.json` (any tracked type — hr/s/hjres/sjres/hconres/sconres), decoded EN+ES, in a **non-terminal** status (`TERMINAL_STATUSES` in `lib/urgency.mjs`: not signed, not vetoed). The moment's reason to exist is that a call can still matter.
2. **Signal rule.** The anchor vehicle appeared, within the last 14 days, in **either** (a) a tier-0 government signal — House/Senate floor-today, bills-this-week schedule, or weekly most-viewed (the lean-free sources `newsdesk.mjs` already treats as citation-grade) — **or** (b) press coverage from **≥2 distinct outlets of non-identical AllSides lean** (`data/media-bias.json`), the exact corroboration rule the newsdesk trigger already enforces. This reuses the pipeline's settled anti-skew guardrail verbatim: trigger sources must stay lean-diverse (press) or lean-free (government), never a single-lean channel.
3. **Action rule.** A concrete callable action exists: the bill page renders, the script endpoint serves it (true for every corpus bill by construction), and the status is one where a staffer tally is meaningful — any non-terminal status qualifies; a committee-stage war-powers resolution is exactly as callable as a floor vote.
4. **Name rule.** The display name follows the naming grammar (§3.3). If no neutral name exists that a reasonable person of either persuasion would accept as a description of the disagreement, the moment does not ship.

Evidence for rule 2 is recorded *in the moment entry itself* (`qualifying_signal` field, §4.1) as links a reviewer can click — tier-0 feed items or the ≥2 articles with outlet + lean. CI validates shape and the mechanical rules (vehicle resolves, non-terminal, EN/ES parity, vocabulary lint); the human review validates truth. No new pipeline persistence is needed for v1 — the newsdesk cache is ephemeral by design and stays that way.

### 3.2 Who writes a moment

**Owner-curated, AI-drafted, human-reviewed — the same posture as every AI artifact on the site, because a moment drives calls.**

- The pipeline (or Claude, in session) may **propose** a moment when tier-0 signals cluster ("three war-powers resolutions touched the floor this week"). Proposal ≠ creation. **No moment is ever auto-created.**
- Claude drafts the entry — name, summary, vehicle one-liners, aliases — in **both languages in the same change** (bilingual-parity hard rule), as a PR against `data/moments.json`.
- **Colby merges.** The existing "Claude opens PRs, never merges" rule *is* the human-review gate, structurally: a moment cannot reach production without owner eyes on the exact rendered strings. This mirrors decode-before-publish — a moment publishes only when its EN **and** ES text have both been reviewed.
- On-page labeling: the moment summary carries the same "AI-drafted, human-reviewed" label as decoded bills. That's honesty labeling, not branding, and it's constitutional.

### 3.3 Nonpartisan by construction

**Naming grammar.** `The ⟨subject⟩ ⟨question|fight|deadline|vote|decision⟩` — a description of a *contention*, never a position, never one side's brand for it. "The Iran war-powers question," "The shutdown fight," "The rescissions vote." One-sided nicknames ("death tax," "amnesty bill") never appear as display text in either language — but they MAY live in the invisible `aliases` search terms (§4.1), from **both** directions, because meeting people at the words they actually use is the whole point of discovery, and an alias renders nowhere.

**Framing rules for the summary (EN and ES alike):**
- Describe the disagreement; never adjudicate it. "Congress is deciding whether…" — then what a yes does and what a no does, in parallel neutral clauses.
- No outcome adjectives (historic, disastrous, commonsense, extreme, radical, dangerous), no motive attribution ("wants to gut," "is trying to protect"), no urgency-theater ("last chance," "before it's too late") — calm confidence is the brand; alarm mechanics are an anti-reference.
- 8th-grade reading level, decoded voice — same register as `ai_summary`.

**Forbidden-vocabulary lint** (mechanical, both languages, enforced by the CI gate in §4.1): imperative advocacy verbs aimed at the reader — fight/resist/stop/save/defend/block and the Spanish equivalents (luchar/resistir/detener/salvar/defender/bloquear) — plus "crisis"/"attack"/"scheme" outside a quoted official title, plus party names used as adversary framing. The lint catches drift; it cannot catch framing — **the owner review is the real gate, the lint is the tripwire.** The list lives in the check script, versioned, so refusals are legible as mechanics.

**Both-directions guarantee.** Each vehicle card states what a yes vote does and what a no vote does where the bill's structure permits; every act link lands on the existing action panel with support and oppose equally available; the moment page carries no stance state whatsoever. No party-coded colors anywhere (existing hard rule); no lean labels on moment pages at all (coverage, and its AllSides labels, stays on bill pages).

**No balance quotas.** Selection is signal-anchored (rule 2), not 50/50-engineered — manufacturing artificial balance is itself a stance. The defensible claim is: *the moment set tracks the government's own record of what's on the floor*, and that record is auditable against the tier-0 feeds after the fact.

### 3.4 Abuse cases to design against

| Abuse / failure | Design answer |
|---|---|
| **Astroturf pull** — pressure (feedback channel, social) to create a moment for a pet cause with no live vehicle | Rules 1–2 are the refusal letter. The criteria are published on a "how moments get made" page, so "no" is legibly mechanical, not editorial. |
| **Asymmetric selection drift** — moments slowly tracking one side's outrage cycle | Signal anchoring: tier-0 sources have no lean; press qualification requires lean-diverse corroboration (the newsdesk rule, reused verbatim). Periodic self-audit is possible because every moment's qualifying evidence is stored in the file. |
| **Naming capture** — adopting one camp's nickname as the display name | Naming grammar + owner review; partisan nicknames confined to invisible aliases, deliberately collected from both directions. |
| **Stale moment as misinformation** — "the shutdown fight" page still implying a pending vote weeks after the CR passed | Settled-state is *computed from vehicle statuses at read/build time* (like urgency — never stored, never trusted to memory), plus a hard `review_by` expiry (§4.3), plus the site-wide KTD-2/AE3 staleness machinery on top. |
| **News-product creep** — "latest developments" sections accreting until Oravan is a newsroom with editorial liability | Structural: the schema has no update-stream field to fill. Anything time-stamped on the page comes from bill data or the sync freshness stamp. |
| **Messaging-bill laundering** — a culture-war moment built on a bill going nowhere, implying a live fight | Rule 2 filters it: a messaging bill without floor/schedule/most-viewed presence or lean-diverse coverage can't qualify. If it *does* get floor time, it is by definition a real callable vote — qualifying it is honest. |
| **EN-only moment slips out** | Same publish gate as decode-before-publish: CI fails the PR if any user-facing field lacks its ES sibling. |

## 4. Data model, page anatomy, lifecycle

### 4.1 `data/moments.json` — static JSON, like everything else

Small (single digits of entries), hand-edited via PR, inline-bilingual like `data/headlines-v2.json` (an overlay file à la `bills-es.json` is wrong here — parity must be checkable per-entry in one place).

```jsonc
{
  "iran-war-powers": {
    "name":    { "en": "The Iran war-powers question", "es": "La cuestión de los poderes de guerra sobre Irán" },
    "summary": { "en": "…", "es": "…" },            // AI-drafted, human-reviewed, framing-rule-compliant
    "aliases": { "en": ["iran", "war powers", "strikes on iran"], "es": ["irán", "poderes de guerra"] }, // search-only, never rendered
    "category": "national_security",                  // one of the 12 in lib/taxonomy.ts
    "vehicles": [
      { "slug": "hconres-45-119",
        "role": { "en": "A yes vote directs…; a no vote leaves…", "es": "…" } }
    ],
    "qualifying_signal": { "type": "tier0_floor",    // tier0_floor | tier0_scheduled | tier0_most_viewed | press
                           "refs": ["https://…"] },  // clickable evidence for the reviewer; press type needs ≥2 refs, distinct lean-diverse outlets
    "opened": "2026-07-23",
    "review_by": "2026-08-06",                        // default opened + 14d; renewed only by a reviewed PR
    "status": "live"                                  // live | retired  (settled is COMPUTED, never stored)
  }
}
```

**CI gate — `scripts/check-moments.mjs`** (same family as the existing check-script culture): schema; every `en` field has an `es` sibling; every vehicle slug resolves in `bills.json` and is non-terminal at merge time; `qualifying_signal.refs` non-empty (≥2 for `press`); `review_by` future-dated at merge; forbidden-vocabulary lint over `name`/`summary`/`role` in both languages; cap of 6 live moments not exceeded.

### 4.2 Page anatomy and surfaces

- **`app/[locale]/moments/[id]`** — SSG both locales, like every other page. Anatomy, top to bottom: neutral name; AI-label + freshness stamp (reusing `StalenessNote`/`lib/freshness-state.ts` — client-side re-diff, so a dead pipeline can't read as eternally fresh); the summary; vehicle cards (existing `BillCard` teaser shape + the yes/no `role` line + link to the bill page and its act flow); a one-line "how moments get made →" link to the published criteria. No comments, no counters, no share-bait.
- **`/moments` index** — lists live moments; renders an honest empty state at zero (the KTD-2 verdict: honesty over fullness — no evergreen filler).
- **Homepage strip** — small, only when ≥1 live moment; disappears at zero. (Homepage is a shared surface: building this needs its own explicit sign-off per standing scope rules; noted here as design intent only.)
- **Search integration** — `BillsBrowser`'s filter additionally matches moment aliases and pins a single moment row above bill results on hit. This is the "Iran" → war-powers hop, at the exact surface where the miss happens today.
- Accessibility as everywhere: semantic landmarks, AA, 44px targets, visible focus.

### 4.3 Lifecycle

- **live** → shown everywhere. Requires: `status: "live"`, `review_by` not passed, ≥1 non-terminal vehicle.
- **settled** (computed, never stored): all vehicles terminal → the page automatically re-renders as "this fight is settled — here's what happened," with vehicles shown in their final statuses. Drops off the homepage immediately; off `/moments` after 14 days. The page itself persists through the end of the Congress — external links must not rot — then retires with the corpus cycle.
- **stale**: `review_by` passed without a renewing PR → honest caveat banner on the page, dropped from homepage and search pinning. This is the zombie-curation tripwire: a moment nobody is tending stops being promoted, visibly, instead of silently aging.
- **retired** (stored): owner decision; page 410s off the indexes. Rare — settling is the normal death.
- **AE3 interplay:** if the whole corpus is stale/dead (`freshnessState`), moments inherit the site-wide caveat — never assert a live fight on dead data. A recurring fight (the next CR) gets a **new** moment; a settled one never reopens (its history is its record).

## 5. Senate nominations — the one true non-bill extension (future, explicitly not v1)

The one recurring call topic with no bill vehicle is a Senate confirmation fight. It earns a sketch because it is the *only* extension with an authoritative structured source: Congress.gov exposes nominations (PN numbers, committee, latest action) via its API — same provider, same key, same sync posture as bills. *(Flagged: I have not verified the current nomination endpoint shape or whether nominations appear in the Senate floor-today feed this session — both need live verification before any build.)*

Sketch: `vehicles[]` gains `{ "kind": "nomination", "citation": "PN123-119", … }`; a small `data/nominations.json` slice synced nightly for referenced PNs only; status model (received / committee / reported / confirmed / rejected / withdrawn / returned) with confirmed/rejected/withdrawn/returned as terminal; decode-before-publish EN+ES; a script-prompt variant for "calling about a confirmation" (support/oppose confirmation — both directions, as ever). Every curation rule in §3 transfers unchanged. **Hard boundary:** nominations only. No executive orders, court cases, or state action — no federal call target, no vehicle, or no structured source. Size L; gated on moments-v1 proving the discovery thesis first.

v1 ≈ **3–5 solo-dev days**. **Marginal cost ≈ zero by construction:** no new runtime endpoints, no new third-party APIs, no new data pipeline; vehicles are already decoded (tier-0 triggers already spend that budget); drafting a moment is a few authoring-time Sonnet calls (cents, inside normal decode spend); a handful more SSG pages is free hosting. The scripts a moment routes to are the existing cached per-bill endpoint — moments concentrate traffic on exactly the top-band bills whose scripts cache best.

## Alternatives briefly noted (rejected)

- **Auto-generated moments from newsdesk clustering** — turns a lean-guarded *refresh* trigger into an unreviewed *editorial* surface; the curation rules exist precisely because naming a fight is the dangerous act. Pipeline proposes, human disposes.
- **Moments as a 13th category / permanent topic hubs** — evergreen topic pages without a live vehicle are advocacy-shaped voids that beg to be filled with commentary. Scarcity and death are features.
- **Coverage (Read section) on moment pages** — duplicates a licensed-content surface (AllSides CC BY-NC posture) for no routing value; bill pages already have it one click away.
- **Separate `moments-es.json` overlay** — parity must be visible per-entry in review; inline `{en, es}` (the `headlines-v2.json` pattern) makes the CI parity check trivial.
- **Stance-parameterized deep links ("call to oppose")** — breaks the both-directions guarantee at the exact moment it matters most.

## Open questions

1. **ES story-naming grounding.** Spanish-language press names stories differently (not translations — "el cierre del gobierno," its own nicknames), and `data/media-bias.json`'s coverage of Spanish-language outlets is thin-to-absent, so the lean-diverse corroboration rule has no ES analog for *naming* input. Default drafting from major ES outlets + owner review works, but whether to formalize an ES source basket (and whether any lean-rating exists for it) genuinely needs an owner call.
2. **Nominations signal source.** Whether nominations surface in the existing Senate floor-today tier-0 feed (giving the §3.1 signal rule a nominations analog for free) or need their own signal — unverifiable without a live check; determines whether the §5 extension inherits the qualification gate cleanly.
