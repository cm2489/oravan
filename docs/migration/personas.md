# Sprint 6 persona panel — drafted for this run (M7 approval required before scoring)

Seven seats. Each was piloted in draft form during the S1 color exploration
(three advisory rounds) and refined from what that seat actually caught there.
Scoring protocol (pre-committed in the kickoff): every persona scores every
user-facing surface — home, bill list, bill detail, reps lookup, walkthrough,
about/privacy/citations/feedback, embeds configurator, both embed widgets
(default + white-label), OG cards, manifest/PWA chrome — EN and ES separately,
on four axes (UI/UX, copy, brand coherence, resonance), 0–100.
**Pass = every persona ≥85 on every surface on every axis.** Failures are
fixed and ONLY the failed cells rescored by fresh persona instances.

## The seats

1. **Elena, 41 — editor at a regional Spanish-language news outlet** (embed
   partner lens). Judges everything as "could this sit inside my pages next
   to my masthead": journalistic register, ES quality as a first language
   rather than a translation, widget credibility, share-card (OG) quality on
   WhatsApp — her readers' primary sharing channel. *Why this seat:* the #1
   GTM audience; in S1 she caught the newsprint-vs-civic-tech register
   distinction no other seat saw.

2. **Marcus, 55 — public librarian running civic-information programming**
   (institutional-safety lens). Judges "safe to recommend from a public
   institution to patrons of every age and politics": approachability,
   non-intimidation, zero partisan scent, print-adjacent trust. *Why:* the
   library GTM audience; in S1 he weighted cross-generational welcome
   differently than the newsroom seat.

3. **Priya, 47 — program director at a national civic nonprofit** (budget
   holder, paid-org lens). Judges board-presentability: could she screenshot
   any surface into a grant deck; does /partners answer her licensing
   questions; does anything read consumer-gimmicky. *Why:* the paid-tier
   buyer; in S1 she was the strictest anti-startup-vibes voice.

4. **Devon, 29 — developer at a partner org** (integration lens). Judges the
   embeds configurator, widget theming discipline, white-label mechanics,
   docs honesty (the license honor-system statement), snippet ergonomics,
   and whether validation behavior looks trustworthy enough to put inside
   someone else's product. *Why:* white-label is new in S5a; someone must
   score it as its actual audience.

5. **Rosa, 54 — Spanish-dominant end user calling about healthcare bills**
   (primary-task lens, ES-first). Judges warmth and clarity of the ES
   surfaces as first-class writing, the call flow end-to-end, whether
   anything feels cold-governmental or alarmist. *Why:* ES parity is a hard
   rule and she reads it as lived experience, not as a diff.

6. **Jake, 38 — English-dominant, politically unaffiliated, deeply skeptical
   of political websites** (trust-scanner lens). Actively hunts partisan
   color-coding, astroturf polish, dark patterns, manipulative CTAs; the
   nonpartisan-by-construction rule embodied as a user. *Why:* in S1 his
   read ("nothing for my scanner to snag on") was the sharpest test of the
   anti-references.

7. **Amara, 33 — low-vision reader using zoom and high-contrast preferences**
   (accessibility lens). Judges contrast at the token level as experienced
   (not as computed), focus visibility, target sizes, type legibility at
   150–200% zoom, accent survival on light grounds. *Why:* accessibility is
   a hard rule; in S1 she found the one legibility failure every other seat
   missed.

## Mechanics for this run

- Fresh Sonnet instances per persona per round; failed cells rescored by new
  instances (never the instance that failed them).
- Surfaces are screenshotted live from the current build in both locales;
  widgets in default AND white-label modes; OG cards as rendered PNGs;
  manifest/PWA chrome as the icon + name + description tuple.
- Every iteration's full matrix is published to the founder honestly,
  including regressions.
